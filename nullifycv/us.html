How it works

Four steps to a bias-free resume

NullifyCV processes your file entirely in the browser. No account, no uploads, no data stored. Works for single files on the free plan and batches of 200 on Pro.

1

Upload your resume

Drop a PDF or DOCX file onto the tool. The file is read directly by your browser — it never travels to any server. NullifyCV works fully offline once the page has loaded.

2

Automatic PII detection

NullifyCV scans the resume for bias signals and personal data: name, email, phone, address, zip code, LinkedIn URL, graduation year, school name, gender pronouns, and more.

3

Redaction applied

Detected PII is covered with solid black redaction bars drawn directly onto the original PDF — same layout, same fonts, same structure. Only the identifying information is removed.

4

Download & share

Download the redacted PDF and share it with your hiring panel. An optional JSON audit log documents every item redacted — suitable for EEOC blind review documentation.

Why blind hiring

Unconscious bias costs companies the best candidates

Research consistently shows that identical resumes receive different callback rates based on name, zip code, and graduation year alone. Structured blind hiring is the evidence-based fix.

📊

The name problem

Studies show resumes with white-sounding names receive 50% more callbacks than identical resumes with Black-sounding names. Removing the name eliminates this bias at the source.

📍

The zip code problem

Zip codes signal race, income, and class. Under CCPA, they're classified as sensitive personal information. Removing zip codes prevents demographic discrimination before it starts.

🎓

The school prestige problem

Ivy League preference systematically disadvantages equally qualified candidates from state schools. Removing school names shifts evaluation to skills, experience, and demonstrated achievement.

The business case: Companies that implement structured blind hiring report more diverse shortlists, reduced legal exposure under Title VII and EEOC guidelines, and stronger long-term team performance. NullifyCV makes the first step — de-identifying the resume — free, fast, and technically verifiable.

EEOC / Title VII

Blind hiring & gender/race discrimination prevention

Removing names, addresses, and demographic signals supports structured blind review processes consistent with EEOC guidelines under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act.

ADEA

Age Discrimination in Employment Act

Graduation years, class years, and early employment dates are removed as age proxies — supporting ADEA-compliant hiring practices for candidates over 40.

CCPA / CPRA

California Consumer Privacy Act — sensitive personal information

Zip codes (precise geolocation proxy), religious affiliations, and racial/ethnic signals are treated as sensitive personal information under CPRA §1798.121.

IL BIPA

Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act

Profile photos are removed in EEOC mode. No facial geometry or biometric data is retained in the output file — supporting BIPA compliance for Illinois employers.
